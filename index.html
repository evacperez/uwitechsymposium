Please Join Us!

Women in Informatics and the iSchool Doctoral Student Association are so excited to be welcoming students, teachers, and mentors from the Seattle region to campus to engage in a range of interactive, innovative tech stations. Students will see the possibilities of using technology for social impact, to understand that they can shape the world and change the world. It is not about what we can do with technology; rather it is engaging with what we should do with technology to make a positive difference in our communities and in society. High School to iSchool Programming

At the iSchool, inclusion and connectedness are celebrated as essential components of academic excellence. With the iSchool now offering freshmen direct admission to the undergrad Informatics program, students with limited early-access to technology are not as competitive as those who have greater access. The iSchool and it's student groups are committed to our High School to iSchool programming series, which includes the iTech Inclusion Symposium and our Spring Hack for Social Good. Through this student-led programming we will actively work to eliminate barriers and obstacles created by institutional discrimination and create opportunities for underrepresented minoritized students.
