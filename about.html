Event Introduction

The Symposium is a half-day event for high school students from all backgrounds and identities to experience equity and inclusion in technology. Engagement comes in the form of tech station activities that provide a fun, hands-on experience with technology and engage students in the field of Informatics (INFO). The intent is to plant the seed and prep high school students to apply for Direct Freshman Admission to INFO. The earlier they learn and get connected, the stronger college applicants can be. We strive to diversify our program and field because information professionals are the people who will make decisions about what future policy, admissions, hiring, funding and products will look like.

The iTech Inclusion Symposium does not require technical experience and will be an opportunity for students to start their journey into the tech field. The event is designed as an accessible, beginner-friendly event to welcome anyone and everyone to come as they are and see themselves in INFO and tech.

What Is Informatics?

The term “informatics” broadly describes the study, design, and development of information technology for the good of people, organizations, and society.

The Informatics major at UW speaks directly to the need for trained and skilled professionals in every sector of society who can turn information into actionable knowledge. We build information systems that work for people!

To learn more about Informatics, check out our iSchool Informatics website.

Event Partners

We are partnering with UW Dream Project, Louis Stokes Alliance for Minority Participation, Brotherhood Initiative, and Women in Informatics, with the intent to feature and celebrate these dedicated on-campus diversity groups. The college students in these diversity groups will provide mentorship to high school participants during the event.

UW Dream Project

A service-learning course and UW program housed out of the UW Seattle campus. The focus is supporting middle and high school students in the greater Puget Sound area with college access and post-secondary planning through near peer mentorship.

The Brotherhood Initiative

A cohort-based program that utilizes research-based strategies to provide multiple pathways for males of color in college to engage in academic, civic, and leadership opportunities, and also access networks and resources that will advance their success.

LSAMP

A program designed to increase the quality and number of students from under-represented minority groups who complete undergraduate degrees in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM), by providing support services at many levels and networks with other LSAMP Scholars.

Women in Informatics

A diversity organization in the UW Seattle iSchool. They are dedicated to empowering women to thrive as producers of technology. They establish and maintain a network of women that offer encouragement, support, and ongoing knowledge that promotes success.
